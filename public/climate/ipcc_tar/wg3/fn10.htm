40 The terms “justice” and “fairness” are often used as synonyms, however, there are debates on the different notions of the terms. Following Albin (1995), justice means distributive justice, in the sense of a general standard for allocating collective benefits and burdens among the members of a community at local, national, or global levels. Principles of justice exist prior to and independently of any phenomenon to be judged. Fairness consists of individual perceptions of what is reasonable under the circumstances, often in reference to how a principle of justice regarded as pertinent should be applied.
