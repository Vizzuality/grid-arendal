EXPERT LEVEL MEETING IN VIENNA
The MCPFE Expert Level Meeting (ELM) on 22-23 October
2001 in Vienna, Austria, discussed important policy issues and
possible formats for the 4
th
Ministerial Conference. The
outcomes highlight key aspects of activity for the MCPFE.
The recent ELM gained a lot of attention with regard to the
preparation of the 4
th
Ministerial Conference. On behalf of Wilhelm
Molterer, the Austrian Federal Minister responsible for Forestry,
Gerhard Mannsberger, Director General of the Forestry
Department, opened the meeting.
Preliminary Conclusions for the 4
th
Conference
The ELM emphasised the significance of forest biodiversity aspects
and of the current draft MCPFE Classification on Protected and
Protective Forest Areas in Europe. Concerning national forest
programmes, the ELM decided that an MCPFE preparatory group
should work further on the preparation of the issue for the
Conference. The ELM underlined the success of the international
workshop on "Forests and Forestry in Central and Eastern
European Countries" (September 2001) and put emphasis on
further work on European co-operation and integration aspects.
Also addressed were economic aspects of sustainable forest
management (SFM), climate change, cultural and spiritual aspects of
SFM and the important role of research. In this respect, inputs will
be prepared by countries and organisations.
Within the discussion on the format for the 4
th
Ministerial
Conference, the ELM brought up differing opinions. The option of
a pan-European policy declaration and the option of resolutions as
well as a combination of both were deliberated.
MCPFE's Working Modalities
The discussion on the working modalities of the MCPFE
confirmed the fundamental principles of voluntariness, flexibility
and a light structure. Regarding specific modifications, the countries
will solicit national consultations as a basis for further discussion at
the next ELM in June 2002.
Lena Yadlapalli

MCPFE SIDE EVENT AT CBD SBSTTA-7
The Seventh Meeting of the Subsidiary Body on
Scientific, Technical and Technological Advice
of the Convention on Biological Diversity (12-
16 November 2001, Montreal, Canada)
discussed forest biological diversity as its main
theme. At an MCPFE side event, the Liaison
Unit Vienna presented the achievements of the
MCPFE. In particular the work on biodiversity
and protected forest areas on a pan-European
level and its link to CBD were highlighted.
WORKSHOP ON PROTECTED FOREST
AREAS IN EUROPE UP-COMING
The recent MCPFE Expert Level Meeting in
Vienna (October 2001) emphasised the
significance of the draft MCPFE Classification
on Protected and Protective Forest Areas in
Europe as well as of the MCPFE work on
biodiversity and forest conservation in general.
It was resolved to convene a MCPFE
Workshop on Protected Forest Areas (PFAs) on
28-30 November 2001 in Køge, Denmark.
The workshop will discuss the results of a
preliminary assignment of national data on
PFAs to the MCPFE Classification.
Furthermore, the participants will exchange
views on selection criteria for PFAs and further
analyse the existing networks of PFAs in
relation to MCPFE requirements. In addition,
the workshop will consider all matters relevant
to biodiversity and forest conservation with
regard to the preparation of the 4
th
Ministerial
Conference on the Protection of Forests in
Europe on 28-30 April in Vienna.

FOCUS ON CRITERIA 2, 4 AND 5
On 24-25 September 2001, the Second MCPFE Workshop on
the Improvement of Pan-European Indicators for Sustainable
Forest Management (SFM) took place in Copenhagen,
Denmark. The lively discussion on national experiences and
ideas on the indicators of Criteria 2 (health and vitality), 4
(biodiversity) and 5 (protective functions) culminated in
valuable recommendations for the Advisory Group.
At the workshop, hosted by the European Environmental Agency,
a broad group of high-level experts from across Europe with
various backgrounds addressed improvements of the indicators on
a pan-European level against their background of practical
implementation.
Proposals for the Improvement of Indicators
The participants discussed the different aspects of health and
vitality of forest ecosystems (Criterion 2) and how to address them
in a more precise way. In addition, the restructuring of the existing
indicators concerning damages was recommended. Regarding
Criterion 4 (biodiversity), the participants agreed to use the draft
MCPFE Classification of Protected and Protective Forest Areas in
Europe. It also was proposed to include,
inter alia,
deadwood and
landscape level diversity as indicators to provide a more complete
set of biodiversity indicators. Addressing protective functions of
forests (Criterion 5), the participants recommended to add
indicators which cover protective functions besides soil and water
protection, e.g. noise and infrastructure. Within the plenary, aspects
related to measurement and reporting details were addressed.

MCPFE Workshop in 2002
A third workshop on 14-15 January 2002 in Budapest, Hungary,
will focus on the quantitative indicators of Criteria 1, 3 and 6, as
well as on the descriptive indicators of all criteria. A final synopsis
workshop will be convened in May 2002. Afterwards the
recommendations for the entire set of improved indicators will be
brought to the MCPFE Expert Level Meeting in June 2002.
