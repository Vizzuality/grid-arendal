National Environment Protection Council

What is the NEPC?

The National Environment Protection Council (NEPC) consists of the environment ministers of each State, Territory and the Commonwealth. It aims to ensure that all Australians benefit equally from environmental protection wherever they live and that business decisions are not distorted nor markets fragmented by variations in major environment protection intiatives between member governments. The Council has law-making powers and works cooperatively to develop National Environment Protection Measures (NEPMs).

The Commonwealth representative on NEPC is the Minister for the Environment and Heritage, Senator Robert Hill.

Background

The National Environment Protection Council was an outcome of an Intergovernmental Agreement on the Environment, which was reached at a Special Premiers Conference in October 1990 and came into effect in May 1992. The agreement provided the basis for a cooperative approach to the management of environmental issues in Australia.

Operation of the NEPC

Each NEPC minister has equal voting power - the Commonwealth Government does not have the power of veto over jurisdictions. Decisions of NEPC can only be made with a two-thirds majority of ministers.

More information on NEPC can be found at the NEPC website.

National Environment Protection Measures

A rigorous consultation process ensures that key stakeholders and interest groups, including industry, environmental groups, government agencies, non-government organisations and members of the public, are involved in making a measure.

Measures may be a combination of:

  • goals - desired essential outcomes to guide management strategies;
  • guidelines on means of meeting desired outcomes;
  • standards - quantifiable characteristics against which environmental quality is assessed;
  • protocols - processes for measuring environmental characteristics to determine whether desired outcomes are being achieved.

The process of making measures results in greater certainty for business, and a greater capacity for environment management industries to address environmental issues across state and territory borders.

Six measures have been made to date:

  1. The Measure for the National Pollutant Inventory (NPI): This Measure is designed to assist environmental management by government, industry and the community by providing improved information on released emissions. Since 1 July 1998 many industrial facilities have been required to estimate and report annually their emissions of NPI listed substances. This information is publicly available on an Internet database.
  2. Measure for Ambient Air Quality: This measure establishes ambient air quality standards and monitoring and reporting protocols for criteria air pollutants, namely CO, SO2, lead, NO2, photochemical oxidants (measured as ozone) and particulates (PM10). The goal of the NEPM is to achieve the national standards set out in the NEPM within 10 years from July 1998.
  3. Measure for the Movement of Controlled Waste between States and Territories: This measure aims to achieve a nationally consistent approach to the tracking of controlled (hazardous) wastes when they are moved interstate for recovery or disposal. The NEPM provides the basis for ensuring that such wastes are properly identified, transported and otherwise handled in ways consistent with environmentally sound practices for their management. Further information about Hazardous Waste Management in Australia can be found at the Hazardous Waste Home Page.
  4. Measure for Used Packaging Materials: This measure provides regulatory support for the National Packaging Covenant, a self-regulatory agreement aimed at improving the recovery, reuse and recycling of used domestic consumer packaging materials. It ensures that participants in the Covenant are not unfairly disadvantaged in the marketplace.
  5. Measure for the Assessment of Contaminated Sites: This measure provides national guidelines for assessing site contamination. The aim of the measure is to provide a nationally agreed and formally recognised approach for assessing site contamination, in order to provide adequate protection of the environment and human health.
  6. Measure for Diesel Vehicles Emissions: This Measure provides guidelines to assist jurisdictions to develop programs to minimise exhaust emissions from diesel vehicles while they are in-service. The Measure provides guidelines on smoky vehicle programs, emission test and repair programs, audited maintenance programs, and engine retrofit and rebuild programs.

Implementation

NEPMs also apply to the Commonwealth. The National Environment Protection Council Act 1994 allows each jurisdiction to chose how best to implement NEPMs. The National Environment Protection Measures (Implementation) Act 1998 and its associated Regulations gives the Commonwealth the power to implement NEPMs on its own land.

Environment Australia has prepared an information package describing how the Act and Regulations affect Commonwealth departments and agencies.

Further Information

The full text of all the NEPMs and information on the development of new NEPMs can be found at the NEPC website.
