This is a summary of forest ecosystem health issues in America's forests. While most of our forests are healthy and productive, there are seven general concerns that we continue to focus on.

Fuel (combustible material) buildups
Invasion of exotic (nonnative) pests
Loss of biodiversity
Wildland/urban interface areas
Air pollution
Degraded riparian areas
Changed disturbance patterns

The "1997 Forest Health Highlights" are provided to give you specific regional examples that illustrate the most important health concerns. These highlights include forest health concerns identified in previous years that continue to be of national interest, some successes we have had restoring forest health, as well as some emerging issues that we are acting on and will continue to monitor. The 1997 Highlights include:

Effects of the Spruce Beetle in Alaska
Asian Long-Horned Beetle Eradication and Urban Forest Restoration
Invasion of the Exotic Plant Miconia in Hawaii
Lake Tahoe Basin: Interface in Danger
Evaluation of Northern Hardwood Decline in the Southern Appalachians
Riparian Degradation and Restoration in the Southwest
Changed Disturbance Patterns East of the Cascades

The Forest Service continues to work to protect ecosystems and to restore them to healthy conditions. Present-day forest conditions took a long time to develop, thus, they will require long-term solutions and commitment.
