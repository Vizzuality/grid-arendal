With 690 cultural and natural sites already protected worldwide, the World Heritage Committee is working to make sure that future generations can inherit the treasures of the past. And yet, most sites face a variety of threats, particularly in today's environement. The preservation of this common heritage concerns us all.




GALAPAGOS ISLANDS, ECUADOR: The delicate ecosystem of the Galapagos Islands is under constant threat. Their listing as a World Heritage Site has led to vigorous legal efforts to control the effects of human habitation, tourism and non-indigenous species.
