TECHNICAL EXCURSION TO THE FEDERAL REPUBLIC OF GERMANY, 2-6 June 2002 
A technical excursion is being organised in the new Länder of Germany on the theme "Forestry and forest industry in the new Länder - a decade after reunification".  Dates are 2-6 June 2002.  Participants will be briefed on policy issues, privatisation, markets and trade between Germany and eastern Europe, and visit a privatised forest enterprise, a large sawmill and value-added processing units (laminated floors and prefabricated houses).

Forest Products Annual Market Review 2000-2001, has just been issued
 
  • Consumption of forest products continued to climb to new records in 2000 in the region covered by the United Nations Economic Commission for Europe (UNECE), despite a slight downturn in North America. 
  • European forest products trade and production accelerated in 2000 in response to strong demand within the EU/EFTA subregion, growing consumption in central and eastern Europe and rising demand outside Europe. 
  • Strong housing construction in North America fostered growth in the panel sector; however, a mid-2000 peak in sawnwood production, combined with a downturn in the paper and pulp sector, may signal an end to the long-term rise in consumption of total forest products.
  • Russian Federation forest products consumption showed the first signs of recovery in 2000, rising 7%, despite significantly higher exports of paper and wood products, boosted partly by a weaker rouble. 
  • Central and eastern European countries forest products markets generally outperformed other subregions of the UNECE, but on substantially smaller volumes.

    Developments in forest policy and institutions, 1998-2000 
    How have governments implemented the commitments they made at Rio in 1992 and Helsinki in 1993?  A recent survey, implemented by the FAO European Forestry Commission shows that over the last few years, nearly all European countries have fundamentally overhauled their forest policies and institutions to bring them into line with the commitments made at the international level to achieving sustainable forest management.   All revised policies aim explicitly at a sustainable balance between the ecological, economic and social roles of forests.  Certification schemes are being developed in many areas.  However, concern is expressed about the economic viability of forest management in Europe.

    Forest certification update for the ECE region, summer 2001 (ECE/TIM/DP/23)
    Over the last year the area of certified forestland has doubled and markets for certified forest products are growing in the UNECE region of Europe, North America and the Commonwealth of Independent States. The ECE Timber Committee follows the market developments for certified forest products and this paper updates the developments in the region and shows current, objective information regarding the status of certification of sustainable forest management and their impacts on forest products markets. This Discussion Paper is not meant to be an all-inclusive source of information on certification. Information in the three previous Discussion Papers, i.e., The Status of Forest Certification in the ECE Region (1998) and Forest Certification Update for the ECE Region, Summer 1999 and Forest Certification Update for the ECE Region, Summer 2000, is not repeated. Rather the authors chose developments to report which have occurred since the last paper.

    Temperate and Boreal Forest Resource Assessment, TBFRA - 2000
    The new UN-ECE/FAO publication "Forest Resources of Europe, CIS, North America, Australia, Japan and New Zealand", known as the Temperate and Boreal Forest Resource Assessment or TBFRA-2000, was released by the mid-year 2000. This study is the most comprehensive set of internationally comparable data on the temperate and boreal forests. It is an important contribution to the Global FRA-2000 and to the set of quantitative indicators of sustainable forest management of the Ministerial Conference on the Protection of Forests in Europe.   More
