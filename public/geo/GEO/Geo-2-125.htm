Written by the independent World Commission for Water in the 21st Century, the Commission Report represents the consensus of its members—experts in water, environment, and public policy. Drawing heavily on the participation of thousands of individuals, whose visions are synthesized in the Vision Report, the Commission resolved to support:

  1. Holistic, systemic approaches based on integrated water resource management.
  2. Participatory institutional mechanisms.
  3. Full-cost pricing of water services, with targeted subsidies for the poor.
  4. Institutional, technological, and financial innovation.
  5. Governments as enablers, providing effective and transparent regulatory frameworks for private action.

Led by Chairman Ismail Serageldin, the Commission frames its resolutions within the four chapters of its Report, which can be viewed—along with the introduction—as PDF files below.
