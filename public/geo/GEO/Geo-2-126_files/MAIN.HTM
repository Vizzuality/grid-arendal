The Second World Water Forum & Ministerial Conference

The World Water Forum did not take its name lightly. It was a truly open and interactive event. A happening and a chance to exchange information and ideas through lively interaction and captivating forms of communication. The Forum proved a platform where participants could influence the process through which ideas were translated into decisions and policy.

A Ministerial Conference was held parallel to the Forum. It aimed to mobilise political support with an eye to countering global water predicaments with concrete action. The ministerial conference was an initiative of the Dutch government.

Also closely linked to the Second World Water Forum was the World Water Fair. A large and varied number of international exhibitors took part, ranging from multinationals to local players, from commercial-minded to idealistic. Thus, visitors to the Fair saw an international community presenting and demonstrating solutions, displays and products on future water use.

Forum, Ministerial Conference and Fair all served one overriding purpose: raising public awareness about the looming water crisis. Through a complete overview of all relevant documents, this site reports on what happened in The Hague. It also looks forward to the Third World Water Forum in Japan in 2003. Through the search engine you will find any specific topics you are interested in. And for those who want to continue the discussions: the Discussion Board welcomes your opinion.
