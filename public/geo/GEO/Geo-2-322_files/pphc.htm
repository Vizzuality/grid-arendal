Palestinian Population and Housing Census

* Background:

    Palestinian Central Bureau of Statistics was entrusted with the census process in 1993 by the Palestinian National Authority (PNA).

    The lack of comprehensive, reliable and scientifically accepted sets of data on population size, composition and characteristics, inherited from the occupation era, is tremendously affecting the activities of various ministries of the PNA.

    Accordingly, PCBS is exhausting its resources, and fostering a series of dialogues with governmental, business, and community groups so that census output will meet user requirements.

* Objectives:

  • To conduct a full enumeration of buildings, housing units, population, and establishments in the Palestinian Territories.
  • To accurately account for population size and composition, at both the national and local levels.
  • To provide potential users with crucially needed data for effective planning, and for the integration of demographic variables in the overall development plan.
  • To obtain a basic set of reliable and updated data on population, buildings, housing units, and establishments in order to enhance the effectiveness of the planning process at all levels.
  • To develop an integrated population information system and master sample frames.

* Census Plan:

  • Collecting census data at the end of 1997, (Completed).
  • Census preliminary counts in the first quarter of 1998, (Completed).
  • Census publication and dissemination at the end of 1998.
  • Analysis of census findings in 1999.
