1. Review of Regional Weather and Smoke Haze

1.1 Dry weather conditions have been experienced over Peninsular Malaysia, central and northern Sumatra and Singapore in the first 2 weeks of March 2002. The dry weather condition has caused an increase in the number of forest fires and associated hot spots over central Sumatra and some parts of Peninsular Malaysia (see Fig 1). Smoke haze has affected many parts of central Sumatra and in central and southern P. Malaysia. Singapore has also been affected but the haze has remained slight. In contrast, no significant haze has been observed over Borneo as weather conditions have continued to be wet over the area. The daily counts of hot spots over the region are depicted in Fig 2.

2. Weather and Haze Outlook

2.1 Except for some afternoon showers over the western coastal areas, generally dry weather is expected over central/northern Sumatra, Peninsular Malaysia and Singapore till the end of March. The prevailing northeast winds are expected to weaken and give way to light and variable winds in the second half of March as the region transits into the inter-monsoon month of April. The hot spots activities are likely to continue to persist till the end of March in view of the generally dry weather.

2.2 Climate forecasts have indicated that the El Nino phenomenon which last appeared in 1997 - 1998 is likely to return in the second half of 2002. However, the prediction so far is for the onset of a weak to moderate El Nino.
