An audience in Sao Paulo watches Secretary of State Colin Powell during his February 14 appearance on MTV's Global Forum. (Photo: Michael Greenwald, U.S. Consulate General Sao Paulo)

Powell Praises Counter-Terrorism Measures Undertaken by Guatemala
Secretary of State Colin Powell appreciates the counter-terrorism measures being undertaken by the Guatemalan government, as well as Guatemalan efforts to fight corruption, and for the progress being made to reduce tensions on Guatemala's border with Belize, says State Department spokesman Richard Boucher. Complete Text

Secretary of State Powell Engages Global Youth Audience via MTV
A young Palestinian woman wonders if her people will ever win the right to return to their homeland. A young Egyptian man wants to know what the United States really knows about Osama bin Laden's involvement with the bombing of the World Trade Center and the Pentagon. An Afghani asks why the United States wasn't paying attention to the abuses of the Taliban before the September 11 terrorist attacks. Complete Text

U.S. Firm, World Bank Sign Deal To
Fight Rural Poverty in Latin America
The private sector arm of the World Bank has signed an agreement with a Florida agribusiness lender for a $20 million credit line to support small and medium agricultural export firms in areas of Latin America suffering from high unemployment and poverty, the Bank announced. Complete Text

2002 National Drug Strategy Seeks
Greater International Cooperation
The 2002 National Drug Control Strategy announced by President Bush February 11 aims to create greater international cooperation in controlling both the demand for drugs in the United States and the supply that comes into the country from foreign sources. Complete Text
