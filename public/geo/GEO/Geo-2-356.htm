VICTIMS AND DAMAGES

Nearly 40 weak-to-moderate earthquakes rattled El Salvador for more than five hours on Tuesday, 8 May,causing no injuries and little damage but sending millions of panic-stricken people scrambling for safety.

The earthquakes, between 2.5 and 4.8 degrees of magnitude on the open-ended Richter scale, were shallow in depth and had their epicenter near San Vicente, 65 kilometers (40 miles) east of San Salvador, the Geo-Technical Research Center said.

Several buildings in San Vicente that were damaged during the stronger earthquakes earlier this year but remained in service, sustained heavier damage this time, leaving hundreds of people homeless.

Two sections of the Pan-American Highway, which links North, Central and South America, caved in near San Vicente, while thousands of residents in Santiago de Maria, 120 kilometers (75 miles) east of San Salvador, were left without electricity. In San Salvador, public buildings and schools were evacuated at the onset of the earthquakes.

Meanwhile, on Wednesday, 9 May, more that 30 aftershocks from Tuesday’s tremors were felt in the area of San Vicente. The inhabitants slept out on the streets for fear of new earthquakes and listened to the moment-by-moment reports of the tremors. Classes have temporarily been suspended.

In the San Jacinto area, the buildings that were the least damaged all toppled to the ground with last Tuesday’s sequence of tremors. Most homes are empty because the inhabitants have not wanted to sleep amidst the debris and therefore went to stay at homes of relatives or friends to be safer.

On 9 May, the President of the Republic, Francisco Flores, carried out a visit to the areas that were the epicenters of the earthquakes. The Head of State committed to support the department of San Vicente in reconstructing homes and setting up temporary housing.

Meanwhile, due to the earthquakes recorded on 8 May, the number of affected persons increased to 1,067 in the city of Mercedes Umaña, department of Usulutan. Forty-four homes were destroyed, and 82 were reported to be seriously damages.

On the 9th of May, the Minister of Public Works announced that the earthquakes that have been felt are direct replicas of January and February’s earthquakes and were a result of the settling of the activated faults.

In the department of Cuscatlan, the Jiboa River has increased its flow by more than two meters in the last 33 days. After the strong quakes on Tuesday, the river rose 11 centimeters, which means the neighbouring villages will become isolated. Therefore, the construction of a canal to evacuate these waters is in progress.

A survey presented on 9 May by the Central American University about the performance of different institutions facing El Salvador’s disaster showed that the international cooperation agencies scored an 8.54 on a scale from 0 to 10, coming in second, after the media. Other top scorers were the assistance organizations, followed by the Armed Forces, the Catholic Church, private enterprise, NGOs and President Francisco Flores with a 7.09 score.

INTERNATIONAL ASSISTANCE TO EL SALVADOR

International assistance to El Salvador continues to be slow, with most funds coming from the International Finance Institutions. The Inter-American Development Bank (IDB) recently provided US$20,000,000 in soft loans for the water sector. For the UN system, the responses are still very limited, with UNICEF being one of the agencies that has received more funds in comparative terms. The UN Development Programme (UNDP), Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO), UN Population Fund (UNFPA), etc, have received less than 40% of their total ceiling as per the international appeal, based on reports given by UN Coordination. The World Food Programme (WFP) has received considerably more, and they are also asking for more food support to complete their global request.

INTER-AGENCY CO-ORDINATION

UN coordination is working well with exchanges of information and cross-cutting issues being resolved at the discussion table. In health, our coordination with Pan American Health Organization (PAHO)/WHO is excellent, particularly in the area of Dengue Fever prevention. Meetings are held every two weeks at present.

UNICEF also continues to provide information to the main donors and allies. During the last two weeks, the Representative visited and exchanged information with the US Embassy and USAID Representative, the Italian Embassy and their technical group helping the Red Cross and the German Embassy and the GTZ group.

UNICEF INTERVENTIONS

Water and Sanitation

After the earthquake recorded on Tuesday, one water treatment system has been badly damaged in Apastepeque. There are 4,000 people without water service between San Nicolas and San Felipe.

Recently, the UNICEF Water and Environmental Sanitation (WES) Emergency Operation has advanced the strategy of providing safe water supplies and sanitation in order to prevent epidemics and water-borne diseases, while returning children and their families to normalcy by replacing their basic services and helping them rebuild dignified homes in safe areas. UNICEF counterparts calculate that to date the UNICEF Emergency WES Operation has directly benefited more than 260,000 people. The principal reconstruction activities recently have been in sanitation, through the rebuilding of permanent latrines in towns and rural villages. The principal counterpart for this activity has been the Ministry of Health, but UNICEF has also played a key role in bringing to bear the installed capacity of non-governmental organizations (NGOs), who have provided personnel and logistics in the field. Plan International, CARE and Medicos del Mundo have been key partners, as have the national NGOs FUSAI and ProVida.

During this reporting period, the Ministry of Health and NGOs have installed more than 900 latrines with support from UNICEF, as follows: In La Paz department, San Juan Nonualco: 160 latrines; San Pedro Nonualco: 150; Santa Maria Ostuma: 100; San Miguel Tepezontes: 60; San Juan Tepezontes: 50; San Emigdio: 40; and San Rafael Obrajuelo: 50. In San Vicente department, Tepetitan: 100. In the major Cafetalón shelter near San Salvador, the last of 200 latrines were completed. The construction of latrine components by the Ministry of Health, with labour and materials financed by UNICEF, continues. Health promoters and NGOs will install these new solutions in the field.

Regarding access to safe water, in this period UNICEF’s principle partner, the national water company ANDA, has installed 14 emergency tanks and bladders in 12 towns, and 5 emergency water treatment plants remain in operation. ANDA has also involved UNICEF as a key partner in their disaster response project, Proyecto Invierno, for which UNICEF is providing emergency tanks and bladders to equip 11 warehouses located in areas throughout the country that are identified at high risk for flooding, landslides and other localised emergencies. Now that the rainy season has arrived, National Aqueduct and Sewer Administration (ANDA) is putting great effort into completing these warehouses and training staff, municipalities and local operators for rapid response. UNICEF has purchased many of these supplies, while others were provided through UNICEF by Sweden, Norway and OCHA.

UNICEF WES is also a key partner to the Tecoluca municipality in their new two-year Development Plan. Besides achieving the insertion of child rights, children in community radios, and education issues into Tecoluca’s Plan, UNICEF has committed to building water supplies and latrines for all the 6,000 earthquake victims of Tecoluca being moved to 8 new settlements. These will be built with funds from Sweden, Spain and European Community Host Organization (ECHO), which are expected shortly. The local NGO ProVida is the executing partner, under guidance of the Ministry of Health and ANDA. In other parts of the country, the Ministry of Health and ANDA have finalised the identification of almost 100 communities that will rebuild aqueducts and receive new wells and latrines with the remaining ECHO funds. Agreements for implementation are being negotiated with the Ministry of Health, ANDA and the NGOs CARE, Plan, PCI, ProVida and FUSAI.

Finally, UNICEF continues to support the Water and Sanitation Network of El Salvador, whose government, NGO and international members are gathering information country-wide to create a new National Water and Sanitation Information System that will finally identify all communities with damaged water and sanitation services, as well as those that have never had the service before. To date, 60% of the country is covered by the data collection. In some departments, more than 70% of information has been gathered (Cabañas, Cuscatlán and Ahuachapán), while one has data for less than 20% of its population (La Paz). This effort should be completed within two months, and will result in a National Water and Sanitation Reconstruction and Investment Plan, led by national government agencies and the El Salvador Network.

Health

Within the national reconstruction plan framework, the Ministry of Health proposed to UNICEF that cooperation be reinforced in a defined geographic area and provide support to the basic system for integral care in the department of San Vicente. The selected area comprises the Guadalupe, Verapaz, municipalities, which were widely affected by the earthquakes.

Although the epidemiological situation of dengue continues to be stable, preventive measures have been reinforced by the Ministry of Health. Within this framework, UNICEF carried out, on 11 May, the delivery to the Ministry of 4 ULV pesticide fumigation pumps, which have the capability to eliminate the dengue vector (1,800 homes per day). This collaboration with UNICEF significantly reinforces the country’s ability to face new epidemics. Contingency actions are being defined together with the Ministry for new cooperation items regarding this issue.

On 10 May, the first case of measles made a terrible comeback to El Salvador. Reports suggest that the virus was introduced from Europe. Immediate measures taken by the Ministry of Health include an urgent information campaign on the issue and the curtailing of its spread by quarantining families of effected children. UNICEF is working, together with PAHO/WHO, in supporting the action of the Ministry of Health in the areas of social mobilization, training of health workers, who are in dear need of refresher training courses on the issue of measles, and provision of materials. There is no evidence at this moment in time that there will be an outbreak.

Guide for the Repair of Common Damages in Infrastructure

Based on the proposal guide prepared by the White Helmet architects, the technical content of the "Guide for the Repair of Common Damages in Infrastructure" is being reviewed by MINED’s Infrastructure Division. Once approved, the material will be published and the Principals of Educational Centres will be instructed as to its use. The guide will help them judge elements of contracting for repair work, for which they are responsible.

Conversations are also underway with the APREMAT project, funded by the European Union, which is interested in supporting this initiative since its objective is vocational middle education.

Educational Materials for Schools and Students

The selection of the materials that will comprise the "Educational Box" for schools has concluded, and the acquisition process for the 1,500 that will be distributed in Educational Centers has begun. Work is also in progress for selecting those that will be recipients for these materials.

The 10,000 educational back packs will have four models: for Preschool, Elementary School, Middle School and Junior High, with the contents of each differentiated according to the students’ academic needs. The acquisition process is underway. It is expected that students from private schools will contribute to the packaging of these back packs.

The number of back packs will be increased by 10,000 in response to a request made by Monseñor Gregorio Rosa Chavez, Archbishop of San Salvador. A coordination mechanism will be set up between MINED, the Catholic Church and UNICEF for the distribution of these materials.

Psycho-Social Intervention

On 10 May, the UNICEF Representative and part of the emergency team visited Apastepeque in San Vicente and some rural zones in the areas where the latest earthquakeic events was particularly strong.

The main objectives of the visit were to meet the mayor from the community, observe the damages of the earthquake recorded on 8 May, and make contact with the Education Directors in order to coordinate the realization of a series of Psycho-Social Rehabilitation methods for children, called Carrousels. This Carrousels will be in 7 different towns on 15, 16 and 21 May. For those events, UNICEF asked for teachers and leaders collaboration.

The Mayor told the UNICEF Representative that the most important damages occurred in some broken buildings and that it was not necessary to established shelters or emergencies roofs.

In San Jacinto there was a meeting at the central park, where the Representative and the Psychologist informed the people about the coordination with the National Secretariat for the Family. Also, all children were invited to participate in next Carrousels.

On 4 May, UNICEF successfully completed a national evaluation of its Psycho-Social Rehabilitation for Children Initiative, the first in the Latin American and Caribbean region. More than 4,000 facilitators for crisis intervention have been trained, including teachers, youth, community leaders, policemen and others. The initiative is being implemented within UNICEF/El Salvador’s emergency cooperation framework, which provides a holistic approach to crisis intervention for children in a broad range of governmental institutions such as the Ministry of Education, Ministry of Health, Family Courts, State Attorney’s Office, National Police Academy, religious organizations, youth groups, NGOs and academic institutions.

El Salvador’s Psycho-Social intervention for children initiative has also embraced a synergistic institutional commitment for emergency preparedness. More than 120 newly trained facilitators, representing the more than 4,000 trained in this model, met to analyze the impact of crisis intervention on children and to exchange experiences in a national context. Follow up mechanisms for guaranteeing children’s mental health and well being include the formulation of institutional policies to guarantee program sustainability, national and international cooperation (for example the UNICEF/Ministry of Education Cooperation Agreement includes the continued training of 35,000 teachers in this model) and a data base. These will ensure continued support for emergency preparedness and post-emergency response.

UNICEF´s Representative in El Salvador, joined by the Ministry of Education, the Secretariat for the Family and Children and the Central American University, highlighted the situation of children in emergencies, natural disasters, or regular programming for child protection. The psycho-social evaluation and relevant recommendations will be published as a national assessment document. This assessment will be presented to government officials and other actors for future programming for emergency preparedness.

Communications/ Information

The alliance proposal with La Prensa Grafica, the newspaper with the widest distribution in the country, produced the desired results. The newspaper will donate the distribution costs of six special sections and will also sponsor the printed advertising. The sections will begin to be published in the third week of June. The preparation of an agreement between UNICEF and La Prensa Grafica is foreseen. Each special 4-page section deals with issues on rights, protection, participation and access to timely information in cases of emergency.

On another front, on 4 May, the official presentation of the photographic exhibit "Give Joy to their Hearts" was made within the Workshop Evaluation Model’s framework. This exhibit of 8 pictures reflects UNICEF’s work within the emergency in the areas of Health, Education, Psycho-Social Rehabilitation and Water and Sanitation.

On 2 May, in the presence of the Minister of Education, the national return to school campaign "School Attendance is a National Commitment" was launched at the National Association of Radio Broadcasters’ Headquarters. With a large audience of media and special guests, UNICEF and the Ministry of Education began this campaign that includes 10 radio spots which are already being broadcast from radio stations throughout the country, a television spot that is now undergoing editing, and a poster for schools. The campaign carrying the slogan "Let there be nobody who can’t attend school… you and I will make it", also includes the participation of youths from the communities, who will act in solidarity brigades going door to door and collecting children that are not attending school.

Together with the Central American University, planning is underway for a video about the issue of Psycho-Social Rehabilitation that will be used during the next training sessions included in this programme.
